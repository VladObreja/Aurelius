Comprehensive Engineering Report: Design, Fabrication, and Logistics for a High-Irradiance Photobiomodulation System (1.5m Body + 1060nm Cranial Module) in the Philippine Archipelago Context
1. Introduction and Environmental Contextualization
The design and fabrication of clinical-grade electro-optical therapeutic devices within a developing archipelagic context presents a unique intersection of biomedical engineering rigor and logistical pragmatism. This report details the technical architecture for a high-irradiance Photobiomodulation (PBM) system, specifically tailored for deployment in Coron, Palawan, Philippines. The project scope encompasses a 1.5-meter full-body panel utilizing standard Red (660nm) and Near-Infrared (850nm) wavelengths, alongside a specialized, modular cranial unit incorporating deep-penetrating 1060nm emitters.
A central constraints analysis reveals that standard Western DIY methodologies—often reliant on readily available extruded aluminum heatsinks and rapid ground shipping—are ill-suited for the Palawan context. Coron, a municipality on Busuanga Island, operates at the distal end of the Philippine supply chain, heavily dependent on maritime freight from Metro Manila. Furthermore, the environmental profile of the region, characterized by high ambient temperatures (tropical monsoon climate, T_{amb} \approx 28^\circ\text{C}-32^\circ\text{C}) and relative humidity exceeding 80%, mandates a fundamental rethinking of thermal management strategies for high-power semiconductor arrays.
This document serves as a comprehensive fabrication dossier, analyzing the photobiological efficacy of selected wavelengths, the electrical topology of Distributed Buck Drive architectures, and the fluid dynamics of active cooling solutions using locally sourced materials. It further addresses the crucial "Gamma" protocol requirement, necessitating precise 40Hz pulsed operation via ESP32 microcontrollers, and provides a robust supply chain strategy leveraging cross-border e-commerce (Lazada, Shopee, AliExpress) to circumvent local component scarcity.
1.1 The Geographic and Logistical Engineering Environment
Engineering in an island municipality requires a "redundancy-first" approach. Unlike Metro Manila, where component failure can be rectified via a trip to Raon (Quiapo) or Deeco Electronics , a failed component in Coron entails a replacement lead time of 2–4 weeks.
1.1.1 Maritime Supply Chain Dynamics
The primary logistical artery to Coron is the sea route from the Port of Manila. Two major carriers dominate this channel: 2GO Travel and LBC Express.
 * Freight Limitations: While air cargo (Cebu Pacific/PAL) is faster, it is prohibitively expensive for heavy, high-density materials like aluminum and transformers. Sea freight is the only economically viable option for the structural components of a 1.5m panel.
 * Vessel Schedules: 2GO vessels such as the St. Francis Xavier operate scheduled routes (e.g., Manila-Coron-Puerto Princesa). Construction timelines must align with these sailing schedules to avoid project stalls.
 * Corrosion Risks: Transit via sea freight exposes components to saline aerosols. All electrical junctions, particularly the non-waterproof terminals of the power supply units (Mean Well LRS series), require conformal coating or enclosure in IP-rated housings upon arrival to prevent premature oxidation.
1.1.2 Material Availability Anomaly
A distinct "availability gap" exists in the Philippines.
 * Construction Materials: Standard construction aluminum—specifically 1/8" x 1" or 2" flat bars (Alloy 6063 or 1100)—is ubiquitous and can be sourced from local hardware depots in Coron or shipped cheaply from Manila suppliers like Aluminum Power Marketing Corp (APMC).
 * Specialized Electronics: High-power LEDs, particularly the 1060nm variety, and specific drivers like the Mean Well LDD-H series are effectively non-existent in brick-and-mortar stores. These must be procured via Shopee Philippines or Lazada, which act as intermediaries for cross-border shipments from Shenzhen/Guangzhou.
2. Photobiological Spectrum Analysis and Emitter Selection
The therapeutic efficacy of the system rests on the precise selection of semiconductor bandgaps to emit photons that align with specific biological absorption peaks. The design calls for a dual-module approach: a systemic body panel and a transcranial penetrator.
2.1 The Systemic Spectrum: 660nm and 850nm
For the 1.5m body panel, the industry-standard pairing of 660nm and 850nm is selected based on the "optical window" of mammalian tissue.
2.1.1 660nm (Deep Red) - Mitochondrial Enhancement
 * Mechanism: Photons at 660nm are absorbed primarily by Cytochrome C Oxidase (CCO), the terminal enzyme in the mitochondrial electron transport chain. This absorption facilitates the dissociation of Nitric Oxide (NO) from CCO, restoring oxygen binding and boosting Adenosine Triphosphate (ATP) production.
 * Semiconductor Chemistry: These emitters utilize Aluminum Gallium Indium Phosphide (AlGaInP) technology.
 * Electrical Characteristics: AlGaInP chips typically exhibit a Forward Voltage (V_f) of 2.2V to 2.6V at 700mA current. This higher voltage drop (compared to IR) impacts the number of LEDs that can be run in a series string on a 48V bus.
 * Thermal Implication: Approximately 70-80% of input power is converted to heat. For a 3W nominal LED driven at 2W, ~1.5W of heat must be dissipated per emitter.
2.1.2 850nm (Near-Infrared) - Deep Tissue Penetration
 * Mechanism: 850nm light penetrates the epidermis and dermis to reach subcutaneous tissue, muscle, and bone. It is less scattered by melanin than 660nm, making it critical for Filipino skin types (Fitzpatrick III-V) where melanin absorption can attenuate visible red light.
 * Semiconductor Chemistry: These utilize Aluminum Gallium Arsenide (AlGaAs) structures.
 * Electrical Characteristics: 850nm LEDs have a notably lower V_f, typically 1.4V to 1.8V.
   * Design Insight: On a 48V driver, one can run significantly more 850nm LEDs in series (approx. 25-28 LEDs) compared to 660nm LEDs (approx. 18-20 LEDs). This efficiency suggests the body panel should be segment-balanced to avoid wasting driver voltage overhead.
2.2 The Cranial Spectrum: The 1060nm Challenge
The cranial module introduces the requirement for 1060nm (1064nm) radiation. This is a non-trivial engineering challenge due to the distinct physical properties of the emitter.
2.2.1 Why 1060nm?
While 810nm is the standard for transcranial PBM (tPBM), 1064nm offers superior penetration through the calvarium (skull) due to reduced scattering coefficients. Furthermore, 1064nm targets water clusters (EZ water) and heat-gated ion channels, offering a thermodynamic mechanism of action distinct from the purely photochemical CCO pathway of 810nm.
2.2.2 The InGaAsP Semiconductor Constraint
Unlike the standard AlGaAs 850nm chips, 1060nm emitters typically rely on Indium Gallium Arsenide Phosphide (InGaAsP) quantum wells.
 * Radiometric Efficiency Drop: A critical insight from the datasheets is the drastic reduction in efficiency. While an 850nm LED might achieve 30-40% wall-plug efficiency, 1060nm LEDs often struggle to reach 10-15%.
   * Thermal Consequence: For every 1 Watt of electrical power driven into a 1060nm LED, nearly 0.9 Watts is generated as waste heat. The cranial unit, despite being smaller, requires a higher density of active cooling than the body panel to prevent junction failure.
 * Cost and Sourcing: These emitters are not available in standard assortments. They must be sourced from specialized semiconductor fabricators on AliExpress (e.g., HotRed or Chanzon stores). The unit cost is approximately 500% higher than standard 850nm chips ($1.50 vs $0.25).
 * Electrical Specs: The V_f for 1060nm is usually lower, around 1.2V to 1.6V , similar to 850nm but often with higher leakage currents at elevated temperatures.
2.3 The "Gamma" Protocol: 40Hz Pulsing Dynamics
The user requirement includes a 40Hz flicker mode, designed to entrain Gamma brainwaves (associated with memory and cognition) via the optic nerve and cortical stimulation.
 * Frequency vs. Duty Cycle: "Gamma" therapy typically utilizes a 50% duty cycle square wave at 40Hz.
 * Driver Compatibility: The selected Mean Well LDD-H drivers specify a PWM dimming frequency range of 100Hz ~ 1kHz.
   * The Conflict: 40Hz is below the datasheet minimum.
   * Engineering Resolution: The 100Hz minimum in the datasheet usually refers to the frequency required to maintain a continuous average current without visible flicker or inductor resonance. However, for PBM, we want the flicker. Driving the Enable/Dim pin at 40Hz simply turns the driver completely ON and completely OFF 40 times a second. The LDD-H rise/fall time is measured in microseconds; it can easily track a 40Hz signal. The only risk is audible whine from the inductors, which can be mitigated by potting or enclosure soundproofing.
3. Power Architecture: The 48V Distributed Buck Topology
To achieve safety, modularity, and efficiency in a humid DIY build, a 48V DC Bus Topology utilizing distributed step-down (buck) conversion is the optimal engineering choice.
3.1 Main Power Supply: AC-DC Conversion
The grid in the Philippines operates at 220V/60Hz. The conversion to a safe DC voltage must happen at a single, well-protected point.
Component Selection: Mean Well LRS-600-48 
 * Output: 48V DC, 12.5A.
 * Total Power: 600 Watts.
 * Rationale: The LRS (Low Profile Enclosed) series is the workhorse of industrial power. It features active cooling (built-in fan) which is essential for Coron's climate.
 * Load Calculation:
   * 1.5m Body Panel: ~250 LEDs @ 2W/LED (derated) = 500W.
   * Cranial Unit: ~40 LEDs @ 2W/LED = 80W.
   * Total: 580W.
   * Headroom: The 600W PSU is running at >95% capacity. This is risky in a hot environment.
   * Recommendation: Split the system. Use One LRS-600-48 for the Body Panel and a separate Mean Well LRS-150-48 (or generic 48V 5A brick) for the Cranial Unit. This prevents a single point of failure and allows the cranial unit to be portable.
3.2 Distributed Regulation: Mean Well LDD-1000H
Instead of using massive Constant Current (CC) drivers (like the HLG series) which run at high voltages (100V+ DC) and pose a shock hazard, we use small DC-DC buck converters for each string of LEDs.
Component Selection: Mean Well LDD-1000H 
 * Architecture: Step-Down (Buck).
 * Input: 9–56V DC (Compatible with 48V Bus).
 * Output: 2–52V DC (Auto-adjusting).
 * Current: Constant 1000mA (1.0A).
 * Efficiency: Up to 97%.
 * Sourcing: Available via Shopee/Lazada from importers. The "Wire" version (LDD-1000HW) is preferred for DIY builds as it eliminates the need for a custom PCB; wires can be soldered and heat-shrinked directly.
String Design Calculations (48V Input):
Buck converters require an overhead voltage (dropout) of ~3V. Max usable output voltage \approx 45V.
 * Red String (660nm): V_f \approx 2.4V.
   * Max LEDs: 45V / 2.4V = 18.75 \rightarrow 18 LEDs in series.
   * Power per String: 18 \times 2.4V \times 1A = 43.2W.
 * NIR String (850nm/1060nm): V_f \approx 1.6V.
   * Max LEDs: 45V / 1.6V = 28.1 \rightarrow 28 LEDs in series.
   * Power per String: 28 \times 1.6V \times 1A = 44.8W.
This high voltage utilization (>90%) ensures the LDD drivers operate at peak efficiency, minimizing driver heat generation.
4. Thermal Engineering: The "Flat Bar" Cooling Solution
A critical deviation from standard PBM designs is mandated by the logistical difficulty of shipping heavy, finned heatsink extrusions to Coron. We must utilize locally available Aluminum Flat Bars, which possess significantly worse thermal performance, and compensate with Active Aerodynamics.
4.1 Thermal Resistance Analysis: Flat Plate vs. Finned
 * The Problem: A flat aluminum plate relies primarily on radiative cooling and laminar convective flow. Its surface area is minimal (just the front and back face).
 * The Math: To dissipate ~400W of heat (generated by a 600W system with ~35% optical efficiency) using natural convection, one would need a surface area of approximately 4 m^2. A 1.5m x 30cm panel only offers \approx 0.9 m^2.
 * The Consequence: Without fans, the flat bars will reach temperatures exceeding 100^\circ\text{C} within minutes, causing LED junction failure (permanent lumen degradation) and potential burn hazards.
4.2 Active Forced Convection Strategy
To make flat bars viable, we must strip the boundary layer of stagnant air using high-velocity airflow.
The "Tunnel" Chassis Design:
 * Mounting: The aluminum flat bars (holding the LEDs) are mounted vertically, spaced 15mm apart.
 * Enclosure: The back and sides are enclosed with plywood or acrylic to form a plenum or "wind tunnel."
 * Forced Air: A bank of 120mm High Static Pressure Fans is mounted on the rear panel, blowing air through the 15mm gaps between the bars.
 * Turbulence: This setup forces air to scour the sides and back of the flat bars at high velocity, drastically lowering the thermal resistance (R_{th}) from \sim 5^\circ\text{C}/W (passive) to <0.5^\circ\text{C}/W (active).
Fan Selection:
 * Requirement: High Static Pressure (measured in mmH_2O), not just airflow (CFM). The fans must push air against the resistance of the crowded bars.
 * Recommended Models (Shopee PH):
   * Arctic P12 PWM: Optimized for pressure.
   * Lian Li ST120: High pressure, robust build.
   * Generic Yate Loon D12SH: Cheap, high speed industrial fans, ideal for a budget build.
4.3 Powering the Cooling Array
The main bus is 48V. Standard computer fans are 12V.
 * Buck Converter: A separate 48V to 12V DC-DC Converter is required.
 * Capacity: 6 fans @ 0.2A = 1.2A. Allow headroom for start-up current. A 10A 48V-to-12V Converter is recommended to run fans + the ESP32 controller. These are readily available on Shopee as "E-bike voltage converters".
5. Control Systems: ESP32 and Gamma Entrainment
The "brain" of the system is the ESP32, chosen for its dual-core architecture and high-precision hardware PWM timers.
5.1 Interface Logic: 3.3V to 48V Isolation
While the ESP32 operates at 3.3V, it controls the Mean Well LDD-H drivers connected to a 48V source.
 * The Connection: The LDD-H DIM pin accepts a PWM signal. A logic "High" (>2.5V) turns the output ON. A logic "Low" (<0.8V) turns it OFF.
 * Direct Drive Risk: While the ESP32's 3.3V GPIO can theoretically drive the DIM pin directly , a failure in the driver could send 48V back to the ESP32, destroying it.
 * Protection Circuit: It is engineering best practice to use an Optocoupler (e.g., PC817) or a simple transistor buffer.
   * Transistor Logic (NPN): Use a 2N2222. Connect ESP32 GPIO to Base (via 1k resistor). Connect Emitter to Ground. Connect Collector to LDD DIM pin.
   * Inversion: When ESP32 is High -> Transistor ON -> DIM pulled Low -> LED OFF. The code must account for this (0% duty cycle = Max Brightness).
5.2 Firmware Implementation for 40Hz
Standard Arduino analogWrite is often fixed at 490Hz or 980Hz. The ESP32's ledc library allows specific frequency setting.
// Gamma Protocol Configuration
const int pwmFreq = 40; // 40Hz
const int pwmResolution = 8;
const int pwmChannel = 0;

void setup() {
  ledcSetup(pwmChannel, pwmFreq, pwmResolution);
  ledcAttachPin(driverPin, pwmChannel);
  // 50% Duty Cycle for therapeutic flicker
  ledcWrite(pwmChannel, 127); 
}

Note: The 40Hz flicker is visibly aggressive. It is recommended to include a physical potentiometer or a Wi-Fi web interface (hosted on the ESP32) to allow the user to switch between "Static Mode" (100% duty) and "Gamma Mode" (40Hz).

7. Operational Safety and Assembly Guidelines
7.1 The Invisible Danger: 1060nm and 850nm
A significant portion of this panel (approx. 55%) emits light that is invisible to the human eye yet carries high radiant flux.
 * Retinal Hazard: The blink reflex, which protects the eye from bright visible light, is not triggered by 850nm or 1060nm. A user could stare directly into a high-power NIR emitter and burn their retina without realizing it until vision loss occurs.
 * Mandatory PPE: Operators must wear safety goggles with an Optical Density (OD) of 5 or greater (OD5+) in the 800nm–1100nm range. Green-tinted lenses are typically used for IR suppression. These should be sourced from reputable suppliers (e.g., Phillips Safety or certified eBay sellers) rather than generic unrated plastic.
7.2 Electrical Safety in a Humid Environment
Coron's humidity poses a risk of tracking faults on the 48V bus.
 * Conformal Coating: It is highly recommended to spray the soldered LED connections on the aluminum bars with a clear conformal coating (acrylic or silicone) or even clear nail polish. This prevents the formation of conductive verdigris (copper oxidation) which can cause leakage currents between the LED pads and the aluminum chassis.
 * Grounding: The Mean Well LRS-600 chassis must be connected to Earth Ground. If the local outlet in Coron lacks a true earth ground (common in older provinces), a dedicated ground rod should be installed or the user must be isolated from the metal chassis.
8. Conclusion
This report establishes that a high-performance, clinic-grade Red Light Therapy system is viable in Coron, Philippines, provided one adheres to a "distributed redundancy" architecture. By leveraging the Mean Well LDD topology, the builder avoids the single-point-of-failure risks of large constant-current drivers. The substitution of extruded heatsinks with active-cooled flat bars solves the logistical bottleneck of sea freight. While the 1060nm emitters introduce cost and efficiency penalties, their inclusion distinguishes this device as a cutting-edge neuro-therapeutic tool. With strict adherence to the 40Hz protocol and OD5+ safety standards, this system represents a pinnacle of DIY biomedical engineering.
9. Second-Order Insights and Future Outlook
 * Grid Independence Potential: The choice of a 48V DC bus makes this system natively compatible with 48V Solar Battery Systems (LiFePO4/Server Rack Batteries), which are increasingly common in Palawan due to grid instability. The system could theoretically run directly from a solar bank, bypassing AC-DC conversion losses entirely.
 * Thermal Harvesting: The waste heat (~400W) generated by the active cooling system is significant. In a clinical setting, this exhaust should be vented; however, in a humid environment, the warm airflow from the fans effectively reduces the local relative humidity around the electronics, self-preserving the device against corrosion.
 * Spectral Broadening: As the 1060nm chips heat up (due to their low efficiency), their peak wavelength will red-shift by approximately 0.3nm/°C. A 40^\circ\text{C} rise in junction temperature could shift the output to 1072nm. This shift is biologically acceptable (water absorption band is broad), but it underscores the absolute necessity of the active cooling tunnel design.
