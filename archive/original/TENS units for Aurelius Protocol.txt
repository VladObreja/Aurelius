2.2 The Waveform Imperative: Asymmetrical Bi-Phasic Square Wave
The biological efficacy of Transcutaneous Electrical Nerve Stimulation (TENS) is dictated by the shape of the electrical pulse.
 * The Requirement: The Aurelius protocol implicitly demands an Asymmetrical Bi-Phasic Square Wave.
 * The Mechanism:
   * Phase 1 (Active/Cathodal): A rapid, high-amplitude positive spike. This is the "depolarization" phase that triggers the action potential in the A-beta sensory fibers of the Posterior Tibial Nerve. It must be a "square" wave to ensure the current rises instantly to the threshold, preventing the nerve from accommodating (getting used to) the signal before it fires.
   * Phase 2 (Recovery/Anodal): A longer, lower-amplitude negative phase. This balances the net charge to zero (Zero Net DC).
 * The Hardware Trap: Many cheap, generic TENS units produce "spiked" or "sawtooth" waves. These are inefficient at recruiting deep nerves like the tibial nerve without causing surface pain (A-delta fiber activation). Furthermore, mono-phasic devices (DC current) pose a risk of electrolytic burns and ion migration under the electrode, which is unacceptable for daily 30-minute sessions.
 * Selection Criteria: All recommended devices in this report (NeuroTrac, Beurer, TENS 7000) utilize the clinically validated Bi-Phasic Rectangular/Square waveform.

Continuous 20 Hz acts as a "metronome," entraining the neural circuitry to a calm, parasympathetic state.

7.1.1 Programming the Beurer EM49

 * Select TENS Mode: Press MENU until TENS is highlighted.

7.1.2 Programming the NeuroTrac Continence
 * Unlock: Remove battery cover. Use a pin to press the hidden lock button if engaged.
 * Select Custom: Press PRG until PC1 (Custom 1) appears.
 * Edit: Press and hold SET.
 * Phase 1 Setup:
   * Mode: Select CONT (Continuous).
   * Hz: Set to 20 Hz.
   * µS: Set to 200 µs.
   * Time: Set to 30 Min.
 * Save: Press ESC.
 * Lock: Press the hidden lock button again to freeze settings.
