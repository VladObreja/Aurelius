# Aurelius Protocol Brief

## Executive Summary

Aurelius is an energy-first, terrain-stabilizing, phase-gated recovery and optimization protocol. It exists to restore usable biological energy as the foundation for action, then progressively restore neuroendocrine, sexual, metabolic, gut-liver, mitochondrial, vascular, sensory, and HFMP function without letting the protocol become an unbounded stack.

The project began as an Excel-centered protocol workspace and research corpus. It is now becoming a versioned knowledge base: structured protocol data, generated human-readable guides, preserved source artifacts, validation reports, and BMAD-inspired protocol-development workflows.

The immediate objective is not to add more interventions. The immediate objective is to finalize the current protocol spine, define how interventions are classified and risk-reviewed, and establish a disciplined path for new research and novel methods to enter the system without destabilizing execution.

## Core Objective

The primary objective is **energy**.

Energy is treated as the basis of all action and the first-order success criterion. Libido, erectile quality, smell, mood, drive, training response, and HFMP expression matter, but they are downstream or parallel expressions of a more fundamental recovery of usable biological energy and resilience.

Energy must be operationalized across several signals:

- perceived daily energy
- crash resistance
- recovery capacity
- motivation/drive
- exercise tolerance
- sleep-restoration quality
- ability to execute normal life without protocol-induced fragility

An energy crash is a top-tier adverse event and should trigger immediate review or rollback.

## Problem

The current protocol addresses a complex cluster involving low energy, low libido, sexual dysfunction, anosmia, fatigue/anhedonia, gut-liver dysfunction, suspected aldehyde burden, fungal terrain, cholestasis/endotoxin pressure, impaired neurovascular signaling, and altered endocrine/neurosteroid dynamics.

The core failure mode is not just biological dysfunction. It is also protocol drift:

- too many plausible mechanisms
- too many candidate interventions
- insufficient distinction between CORE, SUPPORT, EXPERIMENT, PRN, RESET, and ARCHIVE
- weak evidence/safety grading
- unclear rollback logic
- research novelty becoming execution pressure
- simultaneous changes destroying signal quality

The named current biggest risk is **changing too many things**.

## Working Hypothesis

The current terrain hypothesis is that partial ALDH impairment, fungal/gut overgrowth, cholestasis, aldehyde burden, and endotoxin pressure may drive downstream suppression of energy, libido, smell, mood/drive, and sexual function.

This remains a working hypothesis, not dogma. The protocol should identify measurable ways to test either the hypothesis itself or downstream effects.

Potential measurable proxies include:

- stool rhythm, motility, bloating, and GI reactivity
- skin/fungal signs
- bile symptoms: nausea, reflux, RUQ discomfort, stool color/quality
- liver markers where available
- inflammatory markers where available
- organic-acid or aldehyde-adjacent markers where available
- energy response to binder, antifungal, bile-flow, and ALDH-support interventions
- smell, libido, and EQ as downstream neurovascular/neurochemical signals

## Solution Approach

Aurelius uses a phased protocol structure.

### Phase 1.0 - Terrain Stabilization

Primary aim: stabilize gut-liver flow, bowel motility, aldehyde/endotoxin burden, sleep, hydration, and pelvic/autonomic tone.

### Phase 1.5 - Terrain Expansion

Primary aim: deepen gut stability and add ALDH/NAD/Nrf2/metabolic support once Phase 1 is tolerated.

### Phase 2.0 - Restoration

Primary aim: restore neurovascular, endocrine, sexual, sensory, and mitochondrial signaling after terrain is stable.

### Phase 3.0 - Consolidation and Optimization

Primary aim: reduce toward maintenance, preserve function, and use targeted HFMP optimization only where justified.

### Safe Mode - Disturbance Recovery

Primary aim: reduce variables until signal returns after destabilization.

Safe Mode is not failure. It is a required resilience feature.

## Design Principles

1. Energy first.
2. Terrain before force.
3. Phase gates over enthusiasm.
4. CORE means structurally necessary, not merely good.
5. Every intervention is a hypothesis.
6. Every intervention needs an exit.
7. Practices are first-class interventions.
8. Signal quality precedes novelty.
9. Research does not directly change the protocol.
10. Safety must be encoded, not remembered.
11. Complexity must be budgeted even when pill tolerance is high.
12. Rollback is a feature, not failure.
13. Mature success means a quieter protocol.

## Intervention Classification

Executable protocol items should be classified as:

- **CORE:** structurally necessary for the phase objective
- **SUPPORT:** useful under conditions, but not required for the default spine
- **EXPERIMENT:** controlled hypothesis trial with explicit tracking and stop rules
- **PRN:** state-triggered tool
- **RESET:** high-disruption intervention separated from the mainline protocol
- **ARCHIVE:** retained for knowledge, not executable
- **CONTRAINDICATED_FOR_OPERATOR:** known bad fit

Every executable item should eventually declare:

- object type
- phase fit
- primary mechanism
- tier
- intervention force
- evidence grade
- safety grade
- tracking signal
- entry gate
- exit/taper/stop rule
- interaction burden
- complexity cost
- confidence state

## Research Intake

No research domain is categorically ignored at the archive/intake level. However, most research should be ignored by the executable protocol until it passes filters.

Research should flow through:

```text
source
-> claim extraction
-> mechanism mapping
-> relevance scoring
-> evidence grading
-> HIRA / safety review
-> protocol fit decision
-> protocol story
-> canonical data update
-> generated guide update
-> validation
-> commit
```

Possible research dispositions:

- no action
- background knowledge
- supports existing
- contradicts existing
- watchlist
- candidate SUPPORT
- candidate EXPERIMENT
- candidate CORE change
- requires medical review

RAG, when implemented, should assist the Analyst role by retrieving sources and claims. It should not make protocol decisions directly.

## HIRA Requirement

There are no fixed category-level do-not-cross lines for approaches. Instead, higher-risk interventions must pass a formal HIRA review before executable use.

HIRA should score:

- hazard severity
- likelihood
- reversibility
- interaction risk
- monitoring availability
- evidence quality
- operator-specific vulnerability
- medical-supervision requirement
- fallback/rollback clarity

HIRA should produce a risk tier and required safeguards.

Open question: exact HIRA scoring scale and thresholds.

## Evidence Thresholds

Proposed thresholds:

### Body Trial

An intervention may enter a personal trial if it has plausible mechanism, acceptable HIRA, clear dose/exposure, clear stop rule, and no obvious interaction conflict.

### Canonical Protocol Entry

An intervention may enter canonical protocol data if it has evidence grade, safety grade, phase fit, tracking signal, entry/exit logic, rationale, and review notes.

### CORE Promotion

An intervention may become CORE only if it is structurally necessary for a phase, low enough risk for default use, sustainable, tracked, not redundant, and improves phase success.

Open question: final evidence labels and minimum thresholds.

## Safety Authority

The protocol should use layered safety authority:

1. Hard red flags override everything.
2. Energy crash override.
3. HIRA score determines risk tier.
4. Labs are required or recommended for specified intervention classes.
5. Control periods are required after destabilization.
6. Research review informs but does not overrule adverse response.
7. The operator decides within these constraints.

Open question: which intervention classes require medical/lab review before use.

## Rollback Logic

Rollback should depend on the failure class.

Examples:

- **Energy crash:** immediate intervention review, stop new variables, consider Safe Mode.
- **Insomnia/anxiety:** stop stimulatory additions and review dopaminergic/endocrine/overtraining load.
- **Constipation/bile symptoms:** terrain rollback and motility/binder/bile review.
- **Skin/fungal flare:** evaluate antifungal, binder, motility, and herx context.
- **Libido/EQ crash:** review endocrine, neuro, overtraining, sleep, and recent changes.

There should be no single catchall rollback rule. There should be adverse-event classes with response paths.

## Complexity Budget

The operator can tolerate high pill counts, potentially up to 50 pills across 3-4 sessions. This means pill tolerance will not naturally prevent protocol bloat.

Therefore, Aurelius needs an artificial complexity budget based on:

- dosing sessions
- timing windows
- interaction constraints
- tracking burden
- uncertainty/confounding cost
- required monitoring

Complexity should be budgeted because the primary risk is changing too many things, not inability to swallow pills.

## Success Criteria

Aurelius is working when:

- energy improves and does not significantly worsen during protocol execution
- sleep and digestion become stable enough to support restoration
- crash frequency decreases
- the operator can execute the protocol without excessive vigilance
- libido, EQ, smell, mood/drive, and recovery improve as downstream signals
- phase transitions are earned by stability and response, not time alone
- the protocol can shrink toward maintenance

Long-term success means:

```text
maintenance interventions + targeted HFMP optimization
```

The endpoint is not permanent protocol intensity.
