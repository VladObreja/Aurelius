# Aurelius Protocol Spine

This document freezes the current working spine of the Aurelius protocol before deeper BMAD-style optimization.

The spine is not the full research library. It is the minimum executable structure that should remain intelligible to the operator while preserving enough depth to be effective.

## Purpose

Aurelius is a terrain-first, phase-gated recovery and optimization protocol for neuroendocrine, sexual, metabolic, gut-liver, mitochondrial, vascular, and sensory function.

The current focus is recovery from a cluster involving low libido, sexual dysfunction, anosmia, fatigue/anhedonia, gut-liver dysfunction, suspected aldehyde burden, fungal terrain, cholestasis/endotoxin pressure, and impaired neurovascular signaling.

## Design Tension

The protocol must remain:

- simple enough to follow daily
- supple enough to adapt to response
- encompassing enough to address the real system
- constrained enough to avoid stack obesity

## Core Tenets

1. Terrain before force.
2. Minimum effective protocol before maximal stack.
3. Mechanism-led, outcome-corrected.
4. Phase gates over enthusiasm.
5. Stabilize, then stimulate.
6. Practices are first-class interventions.
7. Every intervention needs an exit.
8. Safety is architecture, not afterthought.
9. Canonical data beats chat drift.
10. Evidence tiers, not evidence theater.
11. Operator-centered adaptation.
12. Human guides regenerate from structured source.

## Current Phase Model

### Phase 1.0 - Terrain Stabilization

Primary aim: stabilize gut-liver flow, bowel motility, aldehyde/endotoxin burden, sleep, hydration, and pelvic/autonomic tone.

Core themes:

- binders and bile/endotoxin management
- antifungal entry with caution
- ALDH and sulfur cofactor support
- gentle mitochondrial support
- sleep and nervous-system downshift
- pelvic floor relaxation and mobility

Current CORE agents:

- Taurine
- MSM
- Molybdenum
- Carrot salad
- Psyllium
- Creatine
- Magnesium glycinate
- Glycine
- Undecylenic Acid

Progression signal:

- stools more regular
- nausea absent
- energy modestly improved
- sexual dysfunction beginning to ease
- no worsening of symptoms

### Phase 1.5 - Terrain Expansion and ALDH/NAD/Nrf2 Support

Primary aim: deepen gut stability and add metabolic/ALDH support once Phase 1 is tolerated.

Core themes:

- prebiotic/probiotic terrain correction
- thiamine and NAD support
- Nrf2/ALDH activation
- cautious mitochondrial/dopaminergic support

Current CORE agents:

- Benfotiamine
- PHGG
- MegaSporeBiotic
- Niacinamide

Progression signal:

- bloating and gut reactivity reduced
- sleep stable
- baseline libido flickers returning
- stress tolerance improved
- energy more stable and less crash-prone

### Phase 2.0 - Neurovascular, Endocrine, and Sexual Restoration

Primary aim: restore sexual signaling, androgen/neurosteroid tone, mitochondrial output, vascular function, and sensory response after terrain is stable.

Core themes:

- androgenic and neurosteroid support
- mitochondrial and androgen-receptor support
- gut repair continuation
- microvascular and sensory practices
- cycling to prevent sympathetic overload

Current CORE agents:

- Tongkat Ali
- Cistanche
- Pregnenolone
- DHEA
- LCLT
- TTFD
- Boron
- Tributyrin
- Lactobacillus reuteri

Entry gate:

- stools and sleep stable for 10-14 days
- bile markers/symptoms not worsening

Progression signal:

- morning erections more consistent
- libido clearly improved
- workout recovery good
- mood and drive significantly higher

### Phase 3.0 - Consolidation and Optimization

Primary aim: maintain restored function, refine performance, and selectively use advanced tools without overloading the system.

Core themes:

- sustained libido and EQ
- robust energy under stress
- deload and cycling discipline
- advanced practices and experimental tools only when justified

Current CORE agents:

- Tongkat Ali
- Cistanche
- LCLT
- Pregnenolone
- DHEA

Progression signal:

- sustained high libido and EQ
- robust energy
- low crash frequency
- stable mood and sleep under stress

## Default Operator Practices

Practices are not optional decoration. They are protocol objects with timing, dose, and stop rules.

Current central practices:

- reverse kegels
- psoas release
- adductor and hamstring lengthening
- pelvic mobility drills
- nasal photobiomodulation
- diaphragmatic breathwork
- glute activation
- sleep optimization
- liver/thoracic mobilization
- vagal reset as needed
- hydration baseline

Later or conditional practices include perineal PBM, Angion-style vascular work, sensory conditioning, olfactory training, sauna/cold contrast, fasting, HBOT, and mitochondrial dynamics training.

## Guardrails

- Do not add an item to the executable protocol without a purpose, phase, tier, entry condition, exit condition, and safety note.
- Do not promote SUPPORT or EXPERIMENT items into CORE without an explicit rationale.
- Do not advance phases during unstable sleep, bowel disruption, marked anxiety, worsening bile symptoms, or clear adverse response.
- Do not use the research library as a shopping list.
- When the structured source and human guide disagree, update the structured source and regenerate the guide.
